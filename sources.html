Sources & Citations

All primary sources, secondary sources, and images used in this exhibit are listed here in full. All images are in the public domain or used under Creative Commons licenses.

Primary Sources

Primary Sources

These are original, firsthand documents — the actual records created at the time of the events described in this exhibit.

Primary
"Giving a Dam: Congress Debates Hetch Hetchy." U.S. House of Representatives, Committee on the Public Lands, 63rd Congress, 1st Session, December 1, 1913. Official hearing transcript including Gifford Pinchot's full testimony and the complete Phelan testimony. Published by the U.S. Government Printing Office, 1914. Digitized by History Matters: The U.S. Survey Course on the Web, George Mason University. historymatters.gmu.edu/d/5721
Primary
Muir, John. "Hetch Hetchy Valley." Chapter 16 in The Yosemite. New York: Century Company, 1912, pp. 255–262. Muir's definitive published argument against the dam, written before the final congressional vote. Digitized by the Sierra Club. vault.sierraclub.org/john_muir_exhibit/writings/the_yosemite/chapter_16.aspx
Primary
"Dam Hetch Hetchy!": John Muir Contests the Hetch-Hetchy Dam. Excerpt from The Yosemite (1912). Presented with introductory context by History Matters, George Mason University. historymatters.gmu.edu/d/5720
Primary
The Raker Act (H.R. 7207). Public Law 63-41, 38 Stat. 242. Signed December 19, 1913 by President Woodrow Wilson. Full legislative text granting San Francisco the right to dam the Hetch Hetchy Valley. Archived by Restore Hetch Hetchy. hetchhetchy.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Exhibit-1-The-Raker-Act.pdf
Primary
U.S. Senate Committee on Public Lands. Hetch Hetchy Reservoir Site. Hearing on H.R. 7207, 63rd Congress, 1st Session, September 24, 1913. Washington: U.S. Government Printing Office, 1913. Library of Congress digital collection. loc.gov/item/44020792
Primary
National Archives. "Hetch Hetchy Environmental Debates." Records of the U.S. House of Representatives, 63rd Congress. Includes petitions, resolutions, and citizen correspondence both supporting and opposing H.R. 7207. archives.gov/legislative/features/hetch-hetchy
Secondary Sources

Secondary Sources

These are scholarly and journalistic works that analyze, contextualize, and interpret the primary events.

Secondary
"Frenemies John Muir and Gifford Pinchot." National Endowment for the Humanities, NEH Humanities Magazine. Covers the full arc of the Muir–Pinchot relationship, from their friendship in the 1890s through the Hetch Hetchy split, drawing on John Clayton's Natural Rivals (2019). neh.gov/article/frenemies-john-muir-and-gifford-pinchot
Secondary
Miller, Char. "First Forester: The Enduring Conservation Legacy of Gifford Pinchot." Yale School of the Environment, September 21, 2016. Analysis of Pinchot's utilitarian philosophy, his adaptation of Bentham's "greatest good," and his long-term influence on federal land management. environment.yale.edu/news/article/first-forester-the-conservation-legacy-of-gifford-pinchot
Secondary
Mansfield, G. "The Hetch Hetchy Controversy, 1905–1913." Historia 5 (2018). Eastern Illinois University. A detailed historical analysis of the political maneuvering behind the Raker Act, including the role of San Francisco's lobbying effort and the split within the conservation movement. eiu.edu/historia/5Historia2018GMansfield.pdf
Secondary
"Raker Act." Wikipedia, Wikimedia Foundation. Comprehensive legislative history of H.R. 7207, including political context, opposition, and the act's long-term consequences. Accessed April 2026. en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raker_Act
Secondary
"Gifford Pinchot." Wikipedia, Wikimedia Foundation. Overview of Pinchot's career, philosophy (including his debt to Jeremy Bentham's utilitarianism), role at the Forest Service, and involvement in the Hetch Hetchy controversy. Accessed April 2026. en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gifford_Pinchot
Secondary
"The Greatest Good: A Forest Service Centennial Film — Gifford Pinchot." U.S. Forest Service, U.S. Department of Agriculture. Contextual material on Pinchot's philosophy and the "greatest good for the greatest number in the long run" principle. fs.usda.gov/greatestgood/press/mediakit/facts/pinchot.shtml
Secondary
"Hetch Hetchy: The Epic Environmental Battle That Changed America." More Than Just Parks, September 11, 2024. Comprehensive narrative overview of the Hetch Hetchy controversy and its place in American environmental history. morethanjustparks.com/hetch-hetchy/
Secondary
"History of the Valley." Restore Hetch Hetchy. History of the valley from Indigenous occupation through the dam's construction and present-day restoration efforts. hetchhetchy.org/history-of-the-valley/
Secondary
"Gifford Pinchot (1865–1946)." Forest History Society. Biographical overview covering Pinchot's career, his guiding philosophy, and his legacy in American conservation. foresthistory.org/research-explore/us-forest-service-history/people/chiefs/gifford-pinchot-1865-1946/
